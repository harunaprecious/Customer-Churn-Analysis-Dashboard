# Telecom Customer Churn Analysis (Interactive Dashboard creation using MS Excel)
## Project Objectives
To identify the key factors driving customer churn and provide data-driven recommendations to improve retention.
## Dataset used
<a href="https://github.com/harunaprecious/Customer-Churn-Analysis-Dashboard/blob/main/Telecom-Customer-Churn-Analysis%20Dataset.xlsx">Dataset
## Questions(KPI)
The project aimed to answer the following questions:
•	What is the overall customer churn rate?
•	Which customer segments are most likely to churn?
•	How does customer tenure affect churn?
•	Which products and services are associated with higher churn?
•	What impact does contract type have on customer retention?
    Dashboard interactive view 
## Key Findings
## Overall Churn Rate
•	Total customers: 7,046
•	Churned customers: 1,869
•	Overall churn rate: 26.54%
## Tenure and Churn
•	The highest customer loss occurs within the first six months.
•	Nearly 54.71% of new customers churn during this period.
•	Churn falls to 14.04% for customers who remain for more than two years.
## Customer Segments
•	Customers aged 60 and above recorded the highest churn rate at 36%.
•	Customers under 20 showed the strongest retention.
•	San Diego experienced the highest churn, with 185 customers lost and a churn rate of 64%.
•	Other high-churn locations included Fallbrook and Temecula.
## Services and Products
•	Fiber Optic customers had the highest churn rate at 40.72%.
•	Customers without Online Security or Premium Tech Support were more likely to leave.
•	Customers who subscribed to these services showed stronger retention.
•	Streaming and entertainment services had minimal impact on churn.
## Contract Type
•	Month-to-Month customers had the highest churn rate at 45.84%.
•	One-year contract customers had a churn rate of 10.71%.
•	Two-year contract customers had the lowest churn rate at 2.55%.
## Recommendations
1.	Strengthen customer onboarding
    Implement a structured onboarding and engagement program for new customers during their first six months.
2.	Promote value-added services
    Offer Online Security and Premium Tech Support at discounted rates, especially for Month-to-Month customers.
3.	Investigate high-risk areas
  	Conduct further analysis of Fiber Optic services and high-churn locations such as San Diego to identify service, pricing, or customer experience issues.
## Conclusion
The analysis revealed that customer churn is driven primarily by early customer tenure, contract type, the absence of support services, and specific geographic locations. By focusing on customer onboarding, increasing adoption of retention-supporting services, and addressing issues in high-churn segments, telecom companies can improve customer retention and reduce revenue loss.
